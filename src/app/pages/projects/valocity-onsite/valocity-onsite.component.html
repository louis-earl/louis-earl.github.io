Project Overview

What is Valocity Onsite?

Valocity Onsite is a mobile app that helps property valuers collect data during on-site inspections. It works hand-in-hand with Valocity Connect (our web platform), using the same form via a shared webview so everything stays consistent.

This shiny new app replaced the previous mobile "solution"—a glorified PDF viewer that handled like a soggy clipboard and inspired about as much excitement as a tax audit.

The Goal

To replace outdated workflows (pen, paper, and manual data re-entry)—and the clunky, underused legacy app—with a mobile-first solution that works offline, streamlines inspection flow, and speeds up submissions. The goal was to improve both usability and adoption by building a tool valuers would actually want to use in the field.

My Role

I led the UI/UX design of the app from the ground up. This was my first mobile UX project, and I was the company's first in-house designer on the job. I worked solo at first, then collaborated with a newly hired Lead UX Designer to refine the UI and implement our first-ever design system.

Understanding the Problem

Where we started

The existing mobile app had near-zero adoption. Valuers found it clunky and slower than using paper. (Imagine an app so unintuitive, people preferred handwriting everything in 40-degree heat. That's the level we were dealing with.) The challenge? Build something that actually gets used in the field.

Research Approach

With no direct access to users (target audience was based in India), I got creative. I reverse-engineered the old app by simulating a property inspection in my own home, acting as a valuer.

Key Pain Points:

  • Form was a massive, unbroken scroll with hundreds of fields
  • Errors were hard to locate on submission
  • Field order didn’t match real-world workflow
  • Important inputs hidden under expansion panels

Designing a Better Experience

Design Strategy

I broke the experience into clear, digestible parts:

  • Split the form into logical sections, that match the flow of a real-world inspection.
  • Used a tabbed interface to keep the form manageable
  • Added a progress indicator to show how much was left to complete

Tools & Process

I used Figma end-to-end—from wireframes to interactive prototypes. Our product team demoed these prototypes to partner valuation firms and relayed feedback, which led to:

  • The “map view” feature offering directions to the selected property
  • A final confirmation step
  • Reorganised form structure for speed

Evolving with a Design System

Company Shift

Mid-project, our new Lead UX Designer joined and built Valocity’s first design system. I worked closely with them to:

  • Update my screens with consistent typography, colours, spacing
  • Improve accessibility and cohesion
  • Migrate designs to reusable components

Handoff & Implementation

Design to Dev

I handed off the final designs to a dedicated development team and supported implementation as a designer. While I moved on to other front-end projects, I stayed available to answer questions and adapt the UI based on technical feedback.

Outcome & Reflection

Launch & Impact

Valocity Onsite launched successfully and showed improved adoption compared to the previous app. Stakeholders were enthusiastic—it was the company’s first true in-house design project, and it set the tone for future UX work.

What I’m Proud Of

Designing a full mobile app experience—solo—as a graduate developer. I proved my UX instincts and set the foundation for a new design culture at the company.

What I’d Do Differently

  • Start with a design system from day one
  • Advocate for direct user research, even if remote
  • Collaborate with developers sooner to avoid rework

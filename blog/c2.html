My EDA learning plan

What is your long term goal and/or career pathway?

While my short/medium-term goals are to develop my programming skills and develop myself. Long term, I’d like to get into a career in video game development.

A description of your strengths and limitations in relation to learning

I don’t work best during a typical 9 to 5. I find myself most productive after midday, with extra sporadic bursts of focus and energy during the PM. This inconsistent schedule usually leads to more work having to be completed closer to the deadline. Whether I follow my natural patterns (or even have to go against them!), I usually manage to complete my tasks on time.

A discovery I made from the previous core blog post (identity, values and strengths) was that I tend to second-guess myself and delay the output of my work. I need to work on defining the "good enough" mark and finish there, rather than continuing to work on something until it's perfect (to infinity).

What skills (non-technical - human skills) would you like to see developed in yourself while at EDA?

I want to boost my self-confidence and assertiveness. I want to be able to talk about a topic I am knowledgeable about and not feel embarrassed?! I want to feel comfortable sharing my ideas and feel better about public speaking (I’ll accept I’ll never be a great public speaker, but I can at least get better!).

At this stage, I am a far more passive communicator than an assertive one. Being passive means complying with the wishes of others, always saying yes to others requests without communicating what you think or want. I don’t mean to become an aggressive communicator, not considering other people’s views or feelings, disagreeing or ignoring them. I’d rather be a collaborative one by:

  • Assertive people can communicate without upsetting others or upsetting themselves.
  • Assertive people listen to others and respond appropriately.
  • Assertive people accept their responsibilities.
  • Assertive people appreciate others for their work.

To be fair personal development plan extends past EDA, I’m always wanting to improve these skills by challenging myself!

A commitment to how you will manage your workload in this programme

I use Trello to manage my workload. I set up (at least) three separate lists: Completed, In Progress and To Do. I create Trello cards and slot them into these lists. The further the card is up the list, the higher priority it takes. As I work on things, I move them from To Do, to In Progress, and eventually Completed!

I’m not sure how to explain how my workload is managed safely in relation to others, I’ll answer this via the development of my assertive communication skills:

  1. avoid passive communication by speaking up when something is wrong, letting my ideas be heard and considered.
  2. aggressive communication by listening and thinking about other peoples views, ideas and feelings.

What you will do when things are building up and starting to get to you

Take a moment to unwind. Hang out with friends, call my parents, go for a walk, go and get lunch. One or combination of these things. Taking time away from work sounds counterproductive, but I find there are big benefits to taking a break when stressed: I can clear my mind and sharpen my focus for when I’m ready again.

A commitment as to how and where you will seek help in a timely way

This will be my general process, a tried and true method from my past life studying for my Bachelors!

  1. Use Google, have you heard of it? it’s amazing!
  2. Ask for help in the appropriate Slack channel. If I’m stuck, somebody else in my cohort has been, or probably will be having the same problems too!
  3. Ask my EDA facilitators if all else fails.

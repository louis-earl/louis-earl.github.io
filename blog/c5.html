Core Reflection

What are three new things you have learned about yourself and your ego as a result of the core learning?

My biggest learning about myself is that I should stop neglecting my soft-skills. My biggest strength is probably my ability to learn new technical skills - so perhaps instead of spending time improving something I’m already good at, I should focus on my weaker qualities, emotional intelligence.

One thing I thought was notable was the role neuroplasticity has for learning and refining soft-skills, not just technical skills. I think emotional intelligence and interpersonal skills have become more of a focus for me in this EDA journey than the obvious reason I joined, to learn web development.

I also discovered that I absolutely hate sharing my work. In my first blog post, I discussed how I generally consider work unfinished, even if it’s actually up to a good standard. After finishing a task, I’m usually thinking about what could be done next - either I work on this magical ‘next’ task and discover another ‘next’ task as a result, or, I eventually find a point to stop working despite still having things I want to do to that work. Either way, my work is perpetually in a self-defined state of ‘unfinished’ and there wouldn’t be enough time in the world to complete it. I’m going to learn how to let go and share my work with confidence. The standard of my work doesn’t need to be lowered, just the standards I put on myself.

What are the role of values, empathy and self awareness in learning and programming?

Everyone has their own unique learning style and works at their own pace. It’s important to understand how the pieces of your individuality fit together to form the person you are. By understanding what makes you different, you can nurture your unique path the best way to success. When working with others, you understand your differences are a set of strengths other members may not necessarily have, which can make for a stronger team.

What has surprised you the most about the core learning?

I was surprised at my own previous lack of engagement with my own identity, despite it’s substantial importance to learning and, especially in my case, working in the IT industry.

What were the most challenging aspects of the core learning?

Honestly, I think my biggest struggle was having to talk about myself - I’d call that quite a rare occasion. I think due to the reserved nature of my personality, I prefer to quietly slip into the background and out of the spotlight.

Why do you think we, a programming school, are spending so much time focusing on core learning in a web development bootcamp course?

Because employers are looking for human skills and emotional intelligence before technical skills. Strong interpersonal skills are essential to collaborating with peers in a team, and later in leadership roles. New employees don’t need to have the right technical skills, they need the potential to learn new ones.

Does the time you spent studying core learning here feel like a waste of time? Should you have just used that time to practising programming instead? Justify your answer.

I feel like I still gained a solid understanding of HTML, CSS and JavaScript. The technical content we learned over the last five weeks was substantial. I think by focusing on core learning, I am confident I am better equiped to learn any future technical skills required, as needed.

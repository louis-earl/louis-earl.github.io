Identity, values and strengths

Describe your core personal values

I rarely dive into the black hole that is my own values and identity - it's something that's changed so much for me over the last few years (and I hope it continues to!), so I struggle sometimes to put this into tangible writing. After some thought and reflection, I've pinpointed a few values I'm currently being guided by self-care, friendship/family, respect, enjoyment and achievement.

Self Care

I think self-care is one of my most inner core values - and I hope to explain this is separated from selfishness as much as possible! By practising self-care and replenishing myself internally, especially on a mental health level, I'm able to have more to share with others externally. I picture this is a bit like filling up a jug of water - the fuller the jug, the more cups I can fill up.

I believe the best of my core values all stem from self-care - so by nurturing self-care, all my other values - friendship/family, respect, enjoyment and achievement can all flourish.

I think it's so easy to be overwhelmed with negative thoughts when things aren't going so well. When this gets out of control it affects so many aspects of my life: - Relationships with friends/family - Respect for myself and appreciation of respect others are showing to me - My overall happiness - My desire for personal achievement (especially the smaller things: "Did I get out of bed on time today?", "Did I go for a run today?")

Friends/Family

My family are the people who keep me grounded. An unconditional love that goes both ways. As much as things change in my life (moving cities, changing study, switching jobs), my mother, father, brother, grandparents, aunts/uncles and cousins have all remained a constant - and I'm so lucky for that.

My friends, on the other hand, I treat as the family I've chosen - and I'm so honoured they've chosen me too. I value my friends so highly because I can rely on these people to be supportive without question, especially when all my family are living in the other half of the country.

Respect

As always, respect goes two ways. I appreciate people who treat me with respect, who aren't condescending and make the effort to acknowledge my input. There's a big difference between the people who laugh with you and the people that laugh at you. It's a value I practise because I treat people as I would expect to be treated in return.

Enjoyment

One of the bigger ones for me. I think it's so important to follow your dreams / what you're passionate about in life. And without enjoyment, there is no passion, that spark that you feel when you're doing what you love. For me, I'm incredibly passionate about programming! (especially in a creative environment). I'm so lucky to be able to be doing what I love.

Achievement

Achievement for me means being able to appreciate success every day, especially the little things:

  • Did I go outside for at least half an hour today? Success!
  • Did I study for 25 minutes without getting distracted? Success!
  • Did I get out of bed before 10 am? Success!

To most people, it may seem silly to be celebrating such trivial little things, and they would be absolutely right. However, the reason why I value these achievements is that it gives me motivation. If I can set myself up for success one little task at a time, I can see the path clear to my long-term goals!

Describe your strengths and limitations

I drew from my personality test here because it aligned so well with my own identified strengths and limitations. According to the well known 16-personalities test, I am an INTP-T, also called the "Logician".

A Logician (INTP) is someone with the Introverted, Intuitive, Thinking, and Prospecting personality traits. These flexible thinkers enjoy taking an unconventional approach to many aspects of life. They often seek out unlikely paths, mixing willingness to experiment with personal creativity.

From the following strengths and limitations, I've picked out the ones that best represent me.

Strengths

Imaginative and Original

"These connections are the product of an unrelenting imagination – Logicians’ ideas may seem counter-intuitive at a glance, and may never even see the light of day, but they will always prove remarkable innovations."

I value my imagination and think my creative thinking is what drives passion in what I do. I think there's always more to learn on the road less travelled.

Open-Minded

"Logicians understand they don't know it all – they are highly receptive to alternate theories, so long as they’re supported by logic and facts. In more subjective matters like social norms and traditions, Logicians are usually fairly liberal, with a “none of my business” sort of attitude – peoples’ ideas are what matter."

I've always found it important to keep an open mind. I like to embrace new ideas that aren't my own and encourage people to change my mind. Sometimes this isn't to my benefit, perhaps I may passively say "yes" too much without inputting my ideas. But, mostly to my benefit: it's often better to explore other possibilities than become too fixated on my own thoughts.

Enthusiastic

"When a new idea piques their interest, Logicians can be very enthusiastic – they are a reserved personality type, but if another person shares an interest, they can be downright excited about discussing it. More likely though, the only outward evidence of this enthusiasm will be Logicians’ silent pacing or their staring into the distance."

I couldn't relate to this more - When I'm excited about a thought or idea, I'll rarely show it externally. But on the inside, my brain is exploding with the possibilities.

Limitations

Very Private and Withdrawn

"Logicians are quite shy in social settings. More complicated situations such as parties exacerbate this, but even close friends struggle to get into Logicians’ hearts and minds."

I imagine myself at the centre of a fortress, surrounded by several brick walls. I certainly let people in as I feel closer to them, layer by layer. But I could guarantee very few people have seen the true me at the centre of the fortress. Of course, I'm always working on breaking down these walls! But I make sure I'm comfortable doing so first.

Absent-minded

"When Logicians’ interest is captured, their absence goes beyond social matters to include the rest of the physical world. Logicians become forgetful, missing even the obvious if it’s unrelated to their current infatuation, and they can even forget their own health, skipping meals and sleep as they muse."

I can always find myself drifting off mid-conversation, often my mind will be elsewhere without even realising it. And I think the forgetfulness is quite embedded in who I am. If you told me your name, my brain goes against my best wishes and doesn't seem to find it worth putting to memory. It never fails to embarrass!

Second-Guess Themselves

"Logicians remain so open to new information that they often never commit to a decision at all. This applies to their own skills as well – Logician personalities know that as they practice, they improve, and any work they do is second-best to what they could do. Unable to settle for this, Logicians sometimes delay their output indefinitely with constant revisions, sometimes even quitting before they ever begin."

I can admit my perceived level of skill in most fields is always lower than what it is. I always struggle to share unfinished work. Even worse: what some people would consider "finished" is what I'd call "a work in progress". I usually have so many more ideas that won't benefit for the expense of time to implement them.



I hope you found it interesting learning about me. I certainly gained more from writing this blog post than expected!
